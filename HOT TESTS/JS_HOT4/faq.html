This is the most fun direction to ride it. Let me begin by recommending you don't do this ride unless you're pretty confident in your fitness. Ride to the top and then down and link it directly into Ridegeline (with some double track). Some of the densely covered trails are damp, but still rideable. I've master huck to flat though!

Few sniper rocks on the trail. It's fire road the entire way, absolutely zero singletrack. Check your speed before rumbling over the wet armored section that make a tight right hand turn into a bridge. Drop in off a shallow rock slab and then whip through a few tight berms. Sign is posted at entrance to warn users of features.

Do not ride when wet and not if you are not a skilled rider. Normally most of the hikers don't make it to Otero Upper so that section is a little more clear and you can let it hang out a little more. There is an optional drop 1/2 way down. The trails all run parallel to the Fountain Place paved road so they can be shuttled by car. Logs embedded in the trail have been installed to signal technical terrain.
